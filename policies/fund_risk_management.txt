FUND RISK MANAGEMENT AND REDEMPTION POLICY

Client Risk Assessment Framework:
Client risk appetite is assessed on a scale of 0-100, incorporating:
- Credit spread environment: Widening spreads signal elevated default risk,
  reducing recommended equity allocation by 5-10 percentage points per
  100bp spread widening.
- Employment conditions: Rising unemployment erodes household financial
  security, shifting risk preferences toward capital preservation.
- Market volatility: VIX-equivalent readings above 25 trigger automatic
  portfolio rebalancing toward defensive positions.

Redemption Risk Management:
Fund managers must maintain liquidity buffers of 5-10% of AUM to handle
redemption requests without forced asset sales. During stress periods,
redemption gates may be activated if daily outflows exceed 5% of NAV.

Outflow Triggers and Thresholds:
Historical analysis shows the following outflow acceleration triggers:
- Bond price index decline > 3%: triggers retail redemptions
- Credit spread widening > 50bp in one month: triggers institutional outflows
- Client risk score falling below 40: triggers automatic portfolio de-risking
- Consecutive monthly losses > 3 months: triggers panic-driven redemptions

Inflow Drivers:
Fund inflows are primarily driven by:
- Positive equity returns: Each 1% monthly return generates approximately
  EUR 4-6 million in net inflows for a medium-sized UCITS fund
- Declining interest rates: Investors seek yield in equity and credit funds
- Improving employment data: Higher consumer confidence drives investment

Regulatory Requirements (UCITS):
Luxembourg-domiciled UCITS funds must comply with CSSF requirements for
liquidity risk management, including daily redemption capability and
stress testing of redemption scenarios under adverse market conditions.
